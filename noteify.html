My First Flask and Python Project: Notefy

Building Notefy: A Summer Break Journey into Flask and Python

During a summer break, I embarked on a coding journey that would lead me to create my very first web application—Notefy.

My journey began with a desire into learning the Python programming. Through a comprehensive coding bootcamp on Udemy, I gained a solid grasp of Python's fundamentals, laying the groundwork for my venture into web development.

Eager to apply my newfound Python skills, I turned my attention to Flask, a micro web framework. As I navigated Flask's intricacies, I realized the importance of hands-on learning. Notefy emerged as the perfect project—a platform where I could hone my skills while creating something practical.

Notefy served as my laboratory for experimenting with Flask's capabilities. From handling GET and POST requests to delving into the complexities of Object-Relational Mapping (ORM), each feature added to Notefy deepened my understanding of web development principles.

One of the most enlightening aspects of building Notefy was grappling with hosting considerations. Understanding how to deploy a web application was a crucial step in my journey, enhancing my confidence in navigating the intricacies of web development.

As I reflect on my journey with Notefy, I'm filled with excitement for the adventures that lie ahead in the vast landscape of web development. With Flask, Python, and a sprinkle of imagination, I've only just begun to scratch the surface of what's possible.
